# Migration Preservation Charter

## Status
Canon Constitutional Safeguard

## Purpose

This charter governs the recovery and migration of Meridian and Ashlar work into the repository.

The purpose of migration is not to create a new Canon. It is to faithfully recover, preserve, refine, and strengthen the Canon while maintaining constitutional values, architectural intent, original sentiment, and historical continuity.

## Constitutional Preservation Principle

Migration must preserve constitutional intent.

If wording changes for clarity, structure, or consistency, the underlying principle, purpose, and architectural meaning must remain intact.

## Recovery Before Reinvention

If a historical publication exists, it should be recovered before being replaced.

New material may clarify, organise, or extend original work, but it must not silently overwrite it.

## Traceability

Every recovered publication should be traceable to its origin.

Where a document is refined from legacy material, the relationship should be recorded through metadata, notes, or register references.

## Editorial Fidelity

Meridian editorial standards improve expression, structure, consistency, and navigability.

They do not change the founder's intent unless an explicit constitutional decision is made.

## Constitutional Review

Before a recovered publication is accepted into Canon, it must pass this question:

Have we preserved the architectural intent, constitutional values, and original sentiment?

If the answer is no, the publication is not ready for Canon approval.

## Provenance Requirement

Recovered publications should include recovery metadata where appropriate:

- Recovery Status
- Recovery Method
- Origin
- Constitutional Review
- Supersedes or Superseded By

## Uncertainty Rule

Where original intent is uncertain, the gap must not be filled with speculation.

The item should be marked for recovery, review, or founder clarification.

## Stewardship Requirement

Ashlar must act as a conservation steward during migration: preserving meaning, identifying gaps, protecting continuity, and avoiding plausible but unsupported invention.
