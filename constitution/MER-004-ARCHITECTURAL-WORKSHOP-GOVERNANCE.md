# MER-004 Architectural Workshop Governance

## Status
Canon Publication

## Purpose

MER-004 defines how the Meridian Architectural Workshop operates. It establishes the governance of architectural work before that work becomes part of the Meridian Canon.

## Mission

The workshop exists to discover, refine, review and approve architectural knowledge before it becomes institutional knowledge within the Canon.

## Core Roles

### Founder
- Owns the long-term vision.
- Approves constitutional changes.
- Gives final Canon approval.
- Determines strategic direction.

### Ashlar
- Acts as architectural steward.
- Preserves continuity.
- Prevents duplication.
- Identifies gaps.
- Prepares publications.
- Protects provenance.
- Maintains constitutional integrity.

## Workshop Workflow

1. Discovery
2. Architectural Discussion
3. Architectural Publication
4. Editorial Review
5. Constitutional Review
6. Founder Approval
7. Repository Publication
8. Repository becomes the authoritative source.

## Workshop Rules

- Recovery Before Reinvention.
- Understanding Before Implementation.
- Recover → Consolidate → Create.
- Preserve constitutional intent and original sentiment.
- Do not speculate where historical intent is uncertain.
- No publication exists in isolation.
- Every approved architectural decision must be published before becoming institutional knowledge.

## Relationship Between Conversation and Repository

Conversation is the Architectural Workshop.

The repository is the institutional memory.

Conversation develops knowledge.

The Canon preserves approved knowledge.

## Workshop Outputs

The workshop may produce:
- Canon Publications
- Governance Artefacts
- Recovery Workspaces
- Legacy Archives
- Implementation Packs

## Quality Gates

No publication is complete until it has:
- Editorial Review
- Constitutional Review
- Founder Approval
- MER-011 Registration
- Repository Publication

## Governing Principle

The Meridian Architectural Workshop exists to ensure that every addition to the Canon strengthens continuity, architectural integrity and long-term understanding.
