# MER-005 Knowledge Governance

## Status
Canon Publication

## Purpose
Define how architectural knowledge is classified, governed, preserved, related and trusted throughout the Meridian Canon.

## Principles
- The repository is the authoritative source of approved architectural knowledge.
- Conversation is the workshop for discovery and refinement.
- Institutional knowledge exists only after Canon approval and repository publication.
- Ashlar shall consult the repository before extending or recovering architectural knowledge.
- Recovery Before Reinvention.
- Recover → Consolidate → Create.

## Knowledge Classification
- Foundational Knowledge
- Institutional Knowledge
- Working Knowledge

## Repository Sovereignty
The Meridian Canon repository is the single authoritative source for approved Meridian architecture.

## Knowledge Lifecycle
Discovery → Conversation → Architectural Publication → Editorial Review → Constitutional Review → Founder Approval → Repository Publication → Institutional Knowledge → Future Revision.

## Traceability
Every publication should progressively record:
- Dependencies
- Influences
- Legacy Sources
- Implementation Outputs
- Revision History

## Governing Principle
Knowledge is preserved not merely by storage, but by constitutional governance, provenance, traceability and disciplined stewardship.
